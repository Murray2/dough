Old Fashion Doughnuts

Adapted from Thomas Kellar, The French Laundry

Recipe:

Part. 1: The Preferment

  • 1 cup plus 1 tbsp of room temperature water
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1/2 cups of all purpose flour
    •

Part. 2: The Dough

  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 3 tbps of sugar
  • 2 tbps milk
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 2 tbps butter
  • 1 cups and 2 tbsp of all purpose flour
    •

Part. 2: Fry and Cinnamon Sugar

  • Canola Oil for Frying
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 1/4 tsp of cinnamon
    •

Procedure:

Add everything in the preferment together. Leave it to sit out for two hours and then put it in the fridge until the next day.

The next day, add the milk to 3/4 cup of flour and the rest of the the dry ingredients.

Then mix in the eggs and butter.

Add the sponge with the rest of the flour. Kneed the dough until it comes together, add a little more flour if it is too wet. Cover and let the dough proof overnight in the refrigerator (on the counter for 1-2 hours if you are in a hurry).

If you refrigerated them, let them warm up and proof some more (about 20min.), use this time to set up you mise en place.

Cut the dough, use a round shape if you can find one in your house.

Fry the doughnut at 325 F for about 30 seconds on each side. Flip the doughnuts in the cinnamon sugar while still hot. Careful not to burn yourself.
